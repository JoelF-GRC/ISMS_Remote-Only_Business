# IT Security19 – Acceptable Use Policy
(Annex A: A.5.9, A.5.10, A.5.11, A.5.14, A.8.1)

## Overview
CompanyX provides employees and contractors with information assets — including endpoints, accounts, cloud services, and data — to perform their roles. This policy defines acceptable use of these assets to protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability across CompanyX's remote-first environment.

## Policy

### 1. General Use
(Annex A: A.5.10)
- Company assets (laptops, accounts, cloud resources, SaaS subscriptions) must be used primarily for business purposes.
- Incidental personal use is permitted provided it does not violate other CompanyX policies, consume excessive resources, or create security risk.
- Users are responsible for safeguarding credentials and devices assigned to them.

### 2. Account & Access Use
(Annex A: A.5.10, A.5.15–A.5.18)
- Accounts must not be shared between individuals.
- Users must not attempt to access systems, data, or accounts beyond their authorized role.
- Suspected compromise of an account or device must be reported immediately per IT Security16 (Incident Response).

### 3. Data Handling
(Annex A: A.5.10, A.5.12–A.5.14)
- Information must be handled, labeled, and stored according to its classification as defined in IT Security05 (Asset Management & Information Classification).
- Confidential or Highly Confidential data must not be copied to personal devices, personal cloud storage, or unapproved third-party services.
- Data must only be shared with third parties in accordance with IT Security11 (Supplier & Vendor Management).

### 4. Endpoint & Software Use
(Annex A: A.5.10, A.8.7, A.8.9)
- Only company-managed and approved endpoints may be used to access CompanyX systems and data, in line with IT Security14 (Malware Protection & Endpoint Security).
- Installation of unauthorized software, browser extensions, or unmanaged remote-access tools is prohibited.
- Endpoints must remain enrolled in MDM/EDR and must not have security agents disabled or tampered with.

### 5. Internet, Email & Communications
(Annex A: A.5.10)
- Company email and communication tools must not be used to send Confidential or Highly Confidential information to unauthorized recipients.
- Users must exercise caution with links, attachments, and requests for credentials or sensitive information (phishing).
- Use of company systems to access illegal content, engage in harassment, or conduct unauthorized commercial activity is prohibited.

### 6. Remote Work Requirements
(Annex A: A.5.10, A.7.3, A.7.8)
- Remote work must comply with IT Security12 (Physical & Data Center Security), including securing devices, screens, and workspaces against unauthorized viewing or access.
- Use of public or untrusted networks requires company-approved VPN or equivalent secure connectivity.

### 7. Asset Return & Removal
(Annex A: A.5.9, A.5.11)
- Company assets remain CompanyX property and must be returned upon request or termination of employment/contract, per HR Offboarding Procedures.
- Removal of company assets or data from CompanyX systems requires manager approval.

### 8. Monitoring
(Annex A: A.5.10, A.8.16)
- CompanyX may monitor use of company systems, accounts, and devices for security, compliance, and operational purposes, consistent with IT Security07 (Logging, Monitoring & Security Event Management) and applicable law.
- Users should have no expectation of privacy when using company systems for personal purposes.

### 9. Prohibited Activities
The following are prohibited on CompanyX systems and accounts:
- Circumventing security controls (e.g., disabling EDR, MFA, or logging).
- Unauthorized access to systems, data, or accounts.
- Introducing malware or unauthorized code.
- Exfiltrating Confidential or Highly Confidential data.
- Using CompanyX resources for personal commercial gain or illegal activity.

## Policy Conflicts
Where conflicts exist between this policy and other IT Security policies, the more stringent requirement applies.

## Enforcement
Violations may result in revoked access, disciplinary action, or termination, subject to local laws and HR processes. Violations involving data exfiltration or security circumvention may trigger formal investigation per IT Security16 (Incident Response).
