# IT Security01 – Information Security Management Policy
(ISO 27001:2022 Clauses 4–10, Annex A: A.5.1–A.5.4, A.5.28–A.5.30)

## Overview
CompanyX establishes and maintains an Information Security Management System (ISMS) to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information across its remote-first, cloud-native SaaS environment. This policy outlines the governance framework, leadership responsibilities, risk management approach, and continual improvement requirements of the ISMS.

## Policy

### 1. Context of the Organization  
(Clauses 4.1–4.4, Annex A: A.5.1–A.5.2)
- CompanyX must identify internal and external issues that affect the ISMS.
- A list of interested parties and their information security requirements must be maintained.
- The ISMS scope must be documented and reviewed annually.
- Required ISMS processes, documentation, and interfaces must be defined and maintained.

### 2. Leadership and Commitment  
(Clause 5.1–5.3, Annex A: A.5.1, A.5.2, A.5.3)
- Executive leadership must demonstrate commitment to the ISMS.
- Roles, responsibilities, and authorities for the ISMS must be assigned and communicated.
- Information security objectives must align with the organization’s strategic direction.
- Leadership must promote continual improvement of the ISMS.

### 3. Risk Management and Planning  
(Clause 6.1–6.3, Annex A: A.5.4, A.5.7–A.5.9)
- A documented risk assessment methodology must be used.
- Risks must be evaluated, documented, assigned, and tracked in the risk register.
- Risk treatment plans must reference Annex A controls and feed into the Statement of Applicability (SoA).
- Information security objectives must be measurable and monitored.

### 4. Support and Documentation  
(Clause 7.1–7.5, Annex A: A.6.3–A.6.5)
- Adequate resources must be provided for the effective operation of the ISMS.
- All personnel must receive security awareness training.
- Competency requirements must be defined and tracked.
- ISMS documentation must be controlled, versioned, and retained in accordance with CompanyX requirements.

### 5. Operational Control  
(Clause 8)
- Operational security controls must be implemented, including:
  - Access management
  - Secure development
  - Vulnerability management
  - Vendor risk management
  - Backup and recovery
  - Incident response
- Operational procedures must be documented and periodically reviewed.

### 6. Performance Evaluation  
(Clause 9, Annex A: A.5.28, A.5.30)
- Internal audits must be performed at planned intervals.
- Management reviews must occur at least annually.
- KPIs/KRIs must be defined and monitored (e.g., patch timelines, access review completion rates).

### 7. Improvement  
(Clause 10, Annex A: A.5.29)
- Nonconformities must be documented with corrective actions.
- Root cause analysis must be performed where appropriate.
- Continual improvement must be demonstrated across ISMS processes.

## Policy Conflicts
Where conflicts exist between this policy and supporting procedures, the more stringent requirement shall apply. This policy takes precedence for governance-level matters.

## Enforcement
Noncompliance may result in disciplinary action up to termination. Security incidents caused by policy violations may trigger investigation and remediation actions.
