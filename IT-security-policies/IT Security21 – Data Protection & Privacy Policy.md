# IT Security21 – Data Protection & Privacy Policy
(Annex A: A.5.34, A.5.12, A.5.33, A.8.10)

## Overview
CompanyX is committed to the responsible collection, use, storage, and disposal of personal data. This policy establishes the requirements for protecting personal information (PII) in compliance with applicable data protection regulations including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), as well as CompanyX contractual obligations to customers.

This policy is the primary reference for A.5.34 (Privacy and protection of PII). Data classification is further defined in IT Security05 (Asset Management & Information Classification Policy). Vendor and processor obligations are governed by IT Security11 (Supplier & Vendor Management Policy). Retention and deletion align with IT Security10 (Backup & Recovery Policy).

## Policy

### 1. Scope and Applicability  
(Annex A: A.5.34)
- This policy applies to all personal data processed by CompanyX regardless of format (digital or physical), including:
  - Employee and contractor personal data
  - Customer personal data and data processed on behalf of customers
  - Prospective customer and contact data
- It applies to all CompanyX staff, contractors, and third parties processing personal data on behalf of CompanyX.

### 2. Lawful Basis for Processing  
(Annex A: A.5.34)
- CompanyX must establish and document a lawful basis for each processing activity. Permissible bases include:
  - **Contractual necessity** — processing required to fulfill a contract with the data subject
  - **Legal obligation** — processing required by law
  - **Legitimate interests** — where CompanyX's interests are balanced against data subject rights
  - **Consent** — where required by applicable regulation; must be freely given, specific, informed, and revocable
- Consent-based processing must include a documented withdrawal mechanism.

### 3. Record of Processing Activities (RoPA)  
(Annex A: A.5.34, A.5.9)
- CompanyX must maintain a Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) covering:
  - Name and contact of the controller/processor
  - Purposes of processing
  - Categories of data subjects and personal data
  - Recipients and transfers
  - Retention periods
  - Security measures
- The RoPA must be reviewed at least annually and updated when new processing activities commence.
- The RoPA is maintained in the Compliance Register and is available to regulators upon request.

### 4. Data Minimization and Purpose Limitation  
(Annex A: A.5.34)
- Only personal data necessary for the stated processing purpose may be collected.
- Personal data must not be used for purposes incompatible with the purpose for which it was originally collected.
- Data inputs into third-party or AI tools must be assessed for necessity and restricted to the minimum required for the task.

### 5. Data Subject Rights  
(Annex A: A.5.34)
- CompanyX must fulfill the following rights where applicable under GDPR and CCPA:
  - **Right of access** — provide a copy of personal data held within statutory timeframes (GDPR: 30 days; CCPA: 45 days)
  - **Right to rectification** — correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  - **Right to erasure** — delete data where there is no ongoing lawful basis
  - **Right to restriction of processing** — limit processing pending dispute resolution
  - **Right to data portability** — provide data in a machine-readable format
  - **Right to object** — object to processing based on legitimate interests
  - **Right to opt-out of sale** — CCPA right to opt out of data sale or sharing
- Data subject requests must be logged, triaged by Legal, and fulfilled within required timeframes.

### 6. Privacy by Design and Default  
(Annex A: A.5.34, A.8.25)
- Privacy requirements must be considered at the outset of any new project, product feature, or processing activity.
- Default system settings must not collect more personal data than necessary.
- Privacy impact must be evaluated in design reviews and sign-off processes.

### 7. Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)  
(Annex A: A.5.34)
- A DPIA is mandatory before commencing any processing activity that is likely to result in high risk to individuals, including:
  - Large-scale processing of sensitive categories of data
  - Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas or employee activity
  - Use of new technologies (including AI tools processing personal data)
  - Automated decision-making or profiling with significant effects
- DPIAs must be documented, reviewed by the Privacy function, and retained for audit purposes.

### 8. Data Transfers and Cross-Border Requirements  
(Annex A: A.5.34)
- Personal data must not be transferred to countries outside the EEA (for GDPR) or applicable jurisdiction without appropriate safeguards, including:
  - Adequacy decisions
  - Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  - Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
  - Approved derogations
- Transfers must be documented in the RoPA and reviewed when transfer mechanisms change.

### 9. Data Processors and Third-Party Requirements  
(Annex A: A.5.34, A.5.19)
- Where CompanyX engages third parties to process personal data on its behalf, a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) must be in place prior to any data sharing.
- DPAs must specify:
  - Processing instructions and permitted purposes
  - Technical and organizational security measures
  - Sub-processor approval requirements
  - Deletion or return of data upon contract termination
  - Breach notification obligations
- Third-party processor compliance is assessed as part of the vendor onboarding process defined in IT Security11.

### 10. Data Retention and Deletion  
(Annex A: A.5.33, A.8.10)
- Personal data must not be retained beyond the period necessary for its stated purpose or as required by law.
- Retention schedules must be defined per data category and documented in the RoPA.
- Personal data that has reached the end of its retention period must be securely deleted or anonymized in accordance with IT Security10 (Backup & Recovery Policy) and IT Security05.

### 11. Data Breach Notification  
(Annex A: A.5.34, A.5.26)
- A personal data breach must be reported to the appropriate supervisory authority within **72 hours** of becoming aware of it (GDPR), where the breach poses a risk to individuals.
- Where a breach is likely to result in high risk to individuals, affected data subjects must be notified without undue delay.
- All suspected or confirmed breaches must be reported to the Security Department immediately per IT Security16 (Incident Response Policy).
- Breach records must include: nature of breach, categories affected, approximate number of individuals, likely consequences, and remediation measures taken.

### 12. Sensitive Categories of Data  
(Annex A: A.5.34)
- Processing of special categories of personal data (health, biometric, racial/ethnic origin, political opinion, religious beliefs, sexual orientation, criminal history) requires explicit consent or a specific legal basis.
- Sensitive data must be classified as **Highly Confidential** per IT Security05, subject to the most stringent access controls.

### 13. Training and Awareness  
(Annex A: A.5.34, A.6.3)
- All employees with access to personal data must complete data protection awareness training at least annually.
- Role-specific training must be provided to employees who routinely process PII (e.g., HR, Sales, Customer Success, Engineering).

## Policy Conflicts
Where CompanyX obligations as data processor conflict with customer instructions, Legal must be consulted before proceeding.

## Enforcement
Violations of data protection obligations may result in disciplinary action, regulatory penalties, and reputational harm. CompanyX treats privacy violations as serious incidents subject to the Incident Response process.

## Ownership
Legal / Privacy Function with oversight from the Security Department
