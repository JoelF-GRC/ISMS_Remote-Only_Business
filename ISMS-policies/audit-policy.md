# Internal Audit Policy
ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.2

## Purpose
Ensure the effectiveness and conformance of CompanyX's ISMS through a planned programme of internal audits, providing objective evidence that the ISMS meets the requirements of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and CompanyX's own policies.

---

## 1. Audit Programme

- CompanyX maintains an annual internal audit programme planned at the start of each ISMS year by the ISMS Manager and approved by the CISO/CTO before the audit cycle begins.
- The programme must cover all ISO 27001:2022 Clauses 4–10 and all Annex A control areas at least once per annual cycle.
- Programme planning must account for:
  - The risk level and criticality of the areas to be audited
  - Results of previous audits (repeat findings or major nonconformities increase audit frequency for the affected area)
  - Significant changes to the ISMS, organizational context, technology, or regulatory environment
- High-risk or high-change areas may be subject to additional unscheduled audits within the year.
- The approved programme is documented and communicated to relevant stakeholders before the audit year begins.

---

## 2. Audit Scope and Criteria

- Scope and criteria are defined per individual audit in a written audit plan, confirmed with auditees before fieldwork begins.
- **Scope** must specify: the processes, systems, and organizational areas under review, and the time period covered.
- **Criteria** must reference: ISO 27001:2022 requirements, applicable CompanyX ISMS and IT security policies, legal and regulatory obligations, and relevant contractual commitments.
- Changes to scope or criteria after the audit plan is issued require ISMS Manager approval.

---

## 3. Auditor Competence and Independence

- Auditors must be independent of the area under audit and must not audit their own work.
- Auditor competence requirements:
  - Working knowledge of ISO 27001:2022 Clauses 4–10 and Annex A
  - Familiarity with the specific processes, technologies, or governance areas within scope
  - ISO 27001 Lead Auditor qualification or equivalent experience is preferred for certification-readiness audits
- Internal staff may serve as auditors where independence can be demonstrated.
- External auditors may be engaged where additional independence, specialised expertise, or greater objectivity is required.

---

## 4. Conduct of the Audit

- Each audit begins with an opening meeting to confirm scope, criteria, methodology, and logistics with auditees.
- Evidence is gathered through document review, staff interviews, system observation, configuration inspection, and log sampling as appropriate to the scope.
- Findings are categorised as:
  - **Major Nonconformity** — an ISO 27001:2022 requirement is not fulfilled, or a systemic gap exists that could affect ISMS certification or materially increase risk
  - **Minor Nonconformity** — an isolated or partial gap in meeting a requirement, without systemic failure
  - **Observation / Opportunity for Improvement** — not a nonconformity, but a noted risk, weakness, or area for enhancement
- A closing meeting is held to communicate preliminary findings to auditees before the report is finalised.

---

## 5. Audit Reporting

- The audit report is produced within 10 business days of the closing meeting.
- Each report must include: audit scope, criteria, methodology, period covered, auditor identity, findings with supporting evidence references, and an overall conformance summary.
- The report is distributed to the CISO/CTO and relevant process owners.
- Audit results are a required input to the annual management review per [management-review.md](management-review.md).

---

## 6. Findings, Corrective Actions, and Follow-Up

- All nonconformities are logged in [corrective-action-log.md](../registers/corrective-action-log.md) within 5 business days of report issue.
- Each logged finding requires: root cause analysis, defined corrective action, named owner, and target closure date.
- **Major nonconformities:** corrective action plan due within 10 business days of report issue; a follow-up audit must verify closure within 90 days.
- **Minor nonconformities:** corrective action plan due within 30 business days; closure verified at the next scheduled audit or management review.
- **Observations:** tracked as improvement items in the corrective action log where the ISMS Manager determines follow-up is warranted; not mandatory for formal CA entry unless escalated.

---

## 7. Retention of Audit Records

The following must be retained as documented information per ISO 27001:2022 Clause 7.5:
- Annual audit programme
- Individual audit plans (scope and criteria)
- Audit reports
- Supporting evidence (interview notes, log excerpts, configuration screenshots)
- Corrective action records (maintained in corrective-action-log.md)

Minimum retention period: 3 years, or longer if required by regulatory or contractual obligation. Records are controlled per [document-control.md](document-control.md).
