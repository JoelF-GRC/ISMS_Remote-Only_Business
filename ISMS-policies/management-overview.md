# Management Overview
ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 5 (Leadership and Commitment), Clause 7.4 (Communication)

## Leadership Responsibilities
(Clause 5.1)

Top management must demonstrate active commitment to the ISMS by:
- Approving and endorsing the ISMS scope, objectives, and information security policy
- Ensuring the ISMS is integrated into CompanyX's business processes and strategic direction
- Allocating sufficient resources — budget, personnel, and tooling — for the ISMS to operate effectively
- Communicating the importance of information security and conformance to ISMS requirements to the workforce
- Directing and supporting relevant roles to contribute to ISMS effectiveness
- Promoting continual improvement of the ISMS
- Supporting other management roles in demonstrating their own leadership responsibilities within their areas

## Information Security Policy Approval
(Clause 5.2)

- The information security policy is approved by the CISO/CTO and reviewed at each management review cycle.
- The policy must be appropriate to the purpose and context of CompanyX, include measurable objectives, and be available to interested parties as appropriate.

## Management Review
(Clause 9.3)

The formal management review process — including required inputs, outputs, decision records, and the running review log — is defined and owned by [management-review.md](management-review.md). Management review is conducted at least annually.

## Communication
(Clause 7.4)

CompanyX determines what must be communicated about the ISMS, to whom, when, and by whom:

### Internal Communication

| What | Audience | When | Responsible |
|------|----------|------|------------|
| Information security policy and ISMS objectives | All staff and contractors | At onboarding; when materially updated | ISMS Manager |
| Security awareness updates and alerts | All staff | Annually (training) and as needed for emerging threats | Security Department |
| Audit results and findings | CISO/CTO, relevant process owners | Within 10 business days of audit report issue | ISMS Manager |
| Risk register updates | CISO/CTO, risk owners | At each review cycle or when a significant new risk is identified | ISMS Manager |
| ISMS performance metrics (KPIs) | Leadership, process owners | Quarterly; presented at management review | Security Department |
| Incident notifications and updates | Affected teams, management | During and after incidents per IT Security16 | Security Incident Lead |
| Policy and procedure updates | Affected roles | Upon approval and release | ISMS Manager |

### External Communication

| What | Audience | When | Responsible |
|------|----------|------|------------|
| Personal data breach notifications | Supervisory authority; affected individuals | Per timelines in IT Security21 (72 h for regulatory notification) | Legal / CISO |
| Security incident updates affecting customers | Customers | Per contractual SLAs and IT Security16 | CISO / Customer Success |
| Regulatory and legal compliance reports | Regulators, auditors | As required by applicable law and contract | Legal |
| Third-party security requirements | Vendors and suppliers | At onboarding and contract renewal per IT Security11 | Security Department |
| ISMS certification status | Customers, prospects | Upon request | CISO / Sales |

Unauthorised external communication about CompanyX security incidents is prohibited without CISO and Legal approval.

## Decision-Making

Leadership decisions directly determine risk acceptance, policy approval, resourcing priorities, and the treatment of nonconformities. Decisions made at management reviews are recorded in [management-review.md](management-review.md) and actioned through [corrective-action-log.md](../registers/corrective-action-log.md).

## Ownership
Security Department (ISMS Manager), with top-management accountability held by CISO/CTO
