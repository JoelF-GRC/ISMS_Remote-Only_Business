# ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Certification Readiness Checklist
CompanyX ISMS
Last Updated: YYYY-MM-DD

## Purpose
This checklist tracks the mandatory documented information and operational records required for ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certification. It is intended to be used **after** [gap-analysis.md](gap-analysis.md) has identified priorities, to track concrete progress toward Stage 1 (documentation review) and Stage 2 (operational evidence) certification audits.

## How to Use This Checklist
- **Status**: Missing / Drafted / Approved / In Operation
- **In Operation** means the document is not only approved, but evidence exists that it is being followed (e.g., a register with current entries, completed reviews, logged decisions).
- Stage 1 audits typically require status of at least **Drafted/Approved** for documented information.
- Stage 2 audits require **In Operation** status with evidence/records covering a meaningful period (commonly 3+ months).

---

## Stage 1 vs Stage 2 — What Auditors Check

| Stage | Focus | What "Ready" Looks Like |
|-------|-------|---------------------------|
| **Stage 1** | Documentation review — does the ISMS design meet the standard? | All mandatory policies, the SoA, risk methodology, and scope are documented, approved, and internally consistent. |
| **Stage 2** | Operational effectiveness — is the ISMS actually being run? | Registers are populated and current, at least one internal audit and management review have occurred, controls have evidence of operation (access reviews, logs, patch records, training records, etc.). |
