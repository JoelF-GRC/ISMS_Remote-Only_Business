# Climate Change Addendum  
Applicable to: ISMS Scope, Context, Risk Methodology, BCDR, Supplier Management  
Last Updated: 2025-11-03

## Purpose
To address ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 4.1 requirements regarding environmental conditions — including climate change — and ensure CompanyX evaluates and manages risks arising from climate-related events.

## Climate-Related External Issues
CompanyX recognizes the following climate-related factors may impact its information security, service availability, and suppliers:

- Increased frequency of severe weather events (wildfire, floods, hurricanes)
- Regional power instability impacting cloud regions or SaaS providers
- Disruptions to remote workforce availability (internet outages, local emergencies)
- Transportation and supply chain delays affecting hardware procurement
- Long-term regulatory and compliance obligations related to environmental risk

## Impact on the ISMS
Climate-related threats can affect:

- Cloud infrastructure resilience (regional outages)
- Network connectivity for remote workers
- Business continuity and disaster recovery planning
- Supplier reliability and data center uptime
- Physical security of employee endpoints in affected regions

## Commitments
CompanyX will:

- Include climate-related disruptions in risk assessments (Clause 6.1)
- Review AWS region resilience and geographic diversity
- Validate that critical suppliers maintain climate-resilient operations
- Incorporate severe weather scenarios into BCDR testing
- Update continuity plans based on environmental risk trends
